# Types of Network Topology
# 📖 Overview

A **Network Topology** defines how devices in a computer network are arranged and connected. Depending on the network's size, performance requirements, reliability, and cost, different topologies are used.

Network topologies are broadly classified into:

- **Physical Topology** – Describes the actual physical arrangement of networking devices and communication cables.
- **Logical Topology** – Describes how data flows between devices, regardless of their physical connections.

Apart from these classifications, several commonly used network topologies are used in real-world networking, each with its own advantages, disadvantages, and applications.

# 📚 Types of Network Topology

## 1️⃣ Physical Topology

Represents the **actual physical layout** of devices, cables, and networking hardware.

---

## 2️⃣ Logical Topology

Represents **how data travels** between devices, regardless of the physical layout.

---

## 3️⃣ Bus Topology

All devices are connected to a **single backbone cable** that carries data throughout the network.

---

## 4️⃣ Ring Topology

Devices are connected in a **closed circular loop**, where data travels from one device to the next.

---

## 5️⃣ Star Topology

All devices are connected to a **central switch or hub**, making it the most commonly used topology in modern LANs.

---

## 6️⃣ Tree Topology

A **hierarchical topology** that combines the characteristics of **Star** and **Bus** topologies.

---

## 7️⃣ Mesh Topology

Each device is connected to one or more other devices through multiple communication paths, providing **high reliability** and **fault tolerance**.

---

## 8️⃣ Hybrid Topology

A combination of **two or more network topologies**, designed to meet specific networking requirements.

---

| Star | Central Switch/Hub | High | High | Medium |
| Tree | Hierarchical | High | High | High |
| Mesh | Multiple Connections | High | Very High | Very High |

# 🌍 Choosing the Right Topology

The choice of topology depends on several factors:

- Network size
- Budget
- Scalability requirements
- Reliability
- Performance
- Ease of maintenance
- Fault tolerance

Different environments may require different topologies based on these requirements.

---

# 📌 Key Takeaways

- Network Topologies determine how devices are connected and communicate.
- Physical Topology focuses on the hardware layout.
- Logical Topology focuses on data flow.
- Every topology has its own advantages, disadvantages, and applications.
- Selecting the appropriate topology improves network performance, scalability, and reliability.
