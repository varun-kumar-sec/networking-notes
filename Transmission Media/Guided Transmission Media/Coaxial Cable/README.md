# Coaxial Cable
# 📖 Overview

A **Coaxial Cable** is a type of **Guided Transmission Media** that uses electrical signals to transmit data.

It consists of a central copper conductor surrounded by insulating and protective layers, which help reduce electromagnetic interference (EMI).

Coaxial cables were widely used in early computer networks and are still commonly used today for **television broadcasting**, **broadband Internet**, and **audio/video transmission**.

# 📡 What is a Coaxial Cable?

A **Coaxial Cable** is a guided transmission medium that carries **electrical signals** through a central copper conductor.

It is specially designed to reduce signal loss and electromagnetic interference by surrounding the conductor with multiple protective layers.

Compared to ordinary copper wires, coaxial cables provide better shielding and more reliable communication.

---

# 🏗 Structure of a Coaxial Cable

A typical coaxial cable consists of four layers:

1. **Inner Copper Conductor**
   - Carries the electrical signal.

2. **Insulating Layer (Dielectric)**
   - Prevents the signal from leaking.

3. **Metal Shield (Braided/Foil Shield)**
   - Protects against electromagnetic interference (EMI).

4. **Outer Protective Jacket**
   - Protects the cable from physical damage.

---

# ⚙️ How Does a Coaxial Cable Work?

The communication process is as follows:

1. The sender converts data into electrical signals.
2. The signals travel through the inner copper conductor.
3. The outer shielding prevents external electrical interference.
4. The receiver receives the electrical signal and converts it back into data.

The shielding allows coaxial cables to transmit signals more reliably than ordinary copper wires.

# 📂 Types of Coaxial Cable

## 1. Thicknet (10Base5)

- Thick and rigid cable
- Supports longer transmission distances
- Better signal quality
- More difficult to install
- Used in early Ethernet networks

---

## 2. Thinnet (10Base2)

- Thinner and more flexible cable
- Easier to install
- Lower cost
- Supports shorter transmission distances
- Commonly used in older LAN environments

---

# 🌍 Real-World Applications

Coaxial cables are widely used for:

- Television Broadcasting
- Cable Television (CATV)
- Broadband Internet Connections
- CCTV Systems
- Audio Signal Transmission
- Video Signal Transmission
- Radio Communication

---

# ✅ Advantages

- Better shielding against interference
- Reliable signal transmission
- Higher bandwidth than ordinary copper wires
- Durable construction
- Suitable for audio, video, and communication systems

---

# ❌ Disadvantages

- Bulkier than twisted pair cables
- More expensive than twisted pair
- Difficult to install
- Lower bandwidth than fiber optic cables
- Less commonly used in modern LANs
